Crisis support

If you are in immediate danger, call 999 or go to your nearest A&E.
Do not wait. You are not overreacting.
Please note Counselling Camp is not a crisis service and cannot provide emergency support. If you are struggling to keep yourself safe right now, please contact one of the organisations listed on this page — they are free, confidential, and some are available around the clock.

If you are ready to begin regular counselling, book a free consultation here.

General crisis support

Samaritans

116 123

Free, 24/7, any phone. Email jo@samaritans.org — samaritans.org

Shout

Text SHOUT to 85258

Free 24/7 text support if you'd rather not speak. giveusashout.org

NHS 111

111 (press 2)

24/7 urgent mental health support. Press 2 for mental health — you'll speak to a trained professional. nhs.uk

CALM

0800 58 58 58

Free helpline and webchat for anyone struggling, not coping, or in crisis. Open 5pm–midnight every day. thecalmzone.net

Emergency services

999

If someone is in immediate danger. You are not overreacting. nhs.uk/999

Self-harm & suicide support

PAPYRUS HOPELINE247

0800 068 4141

For anyone under 35 experiencing thoughts of suicide, or anyone concerned about a young person. Free, confidential, 24/7. Text 88247 or email pat@papyrus-uk.org — papyrus-uk.org

Mind

0300 123 3393

Mental health information and support. Mon–Fri 9am–6pm. mind.org.uk

Harmless

Email support

Support for people who self-harm and those who care for them. admin@harmless.org.uk — harmless.org.uk
